The Impact of Social Media on K-POP Globalisation

Over the past 20 years, the music industry has witnessed significant changes, especially within the realm of K-pop. How did K-pop manage to evolve from a niche music market to a major global force?

In 2012, the music video for "Gangnam Style" was uploaded to YouTube and garnered approximately 500,000 views on its first day (Walfisz, 2023). Particularly, Psy's entertaining dance captured significant attention, prompting people to rapidly share this amusing content through social media. As a result of this viral phenomenon, the music quickly gained worldwide popularity, signalling the onset of K-pop's global expansion.

In response to this trend, K-pop companies actively began leveraging social media platforms such as YouTube, Twitter, and Instagram as integral components of their marketing strategies (Cai, 2021b). Social media enabled direct communication between artists and fans, with artists utilising these platforms for daily updates, behind-the-scenes insights, and interactive engagement (the Harvard Crimson, 2013). Furthermore, fans extensively utilised social media, fostering a sense of community and emerging as staunch supporters of their favourite K-pop artists.

Consequently, K-pop transcended traditional boundaries with the advent of social media. In the era of the digital revolution, where content dissemination became more accessible, social media platforms emerged as indispensable tools for facilitating cultural exchange, thereby playing a pivotal role in expanding the influence of K-pop beyond geographical constraints (Writer, 2023c).

Global impact of K-POP's rise

Examining the comprehensive web search data reveals a remarkable increase in the global prominence of K-pop around 2010, with this trend persisting and gradually increasing until the latter part of 2020. In contrast to the past, where traditional mediums such as television and radio held sway, today, the rise of social media platforms as central hubs for music exploration and fan interaction is undeniable. This surge in online search volume highlights their profound impact.

Analysis of K-POP search trends on YouTube

YouTube, the world's second most popular search engine after Google, stands as the ultimate social media platform for connecting with a global audience. Entertainment serves as a primary driver for its widespread usage, with roughly 96% of internet users across approximately 100 countries actively engaging with its content (Blogger, 2024). Notably, K-pop transcends geographical barriers, garnering significant viewership even in far-flung corners of the globe, a testament to its universal appeal.

K-Pop entries in the Billboard charts

Since its entry into the US market in 2009 and its subsequent rise on the Billboard charts, K-pop was still largely considered a niche genre on a global scale. However, in 2012, Psy's "Gangnam Style" became a sensation on YouTube, marking the beginning of increased global awareness of K-pop. Then, in 2017, with BTS's breakthrough in the United States, K-pop's popularity became undeniable on the Billboard charts. In comparison to the past, many K-pop artists are now making significant strides on the Billboard charts, and through various social media platforms, K-pop's visibility has been globalised, spearheaded by YouTube.

K-POP's popularity on social media

Before the advent of social media platforms, people depended solely on television, radio, or word of mouth to assess an artist's popularity. However, in today's era, with the rapid surge in social media usage, it has become possible to gauge popularity through search terms on social media platforms. In 2022, BTS was notably mentioned more frequently than other artists across three social media platforms: Twitter, Tumblr, and Reddit.

How do K-pop fans keep up with K-pop news

People seek information about their favourite K-pop artists through diverse channels, yet social media stands out for its ability to provide real-time updates. Acting as a pivotal platform, social media ensures that K-pop enthusiasts stay updated with the latest developments, fostering broader awareness of the genre. In this manner, social media not only facilitates interaction between fans and artists but also connects fans globally, leveraging online communities to propel K-pop worldwide.

The combination of social media and K-pop is not just a cultural phenomenon, but a revolution in connectivity. It demonstrated how social media platforms are seamlessly intertwined with art, opening avenues for global interactions and understanding. Through the power of social media, K-pop has transcended geographic boundaries, captivating audiences around the world, and helped create a sense of unity and belonging among fans across continents.
By showcasing how K-pop used social media to become a successful global phenomenon, we were able to confirm that people can have a positive influence by breaking down boundaries and sharing something even in different environments and cultures. As such, we must leverage social media as a positive tool, harnessing its power to spread joy, interconnectedness and cultural understanding.
